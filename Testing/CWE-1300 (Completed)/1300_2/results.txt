4. CWE-Driven Review
CWE-226:
    - key_reg holds secret key is never explicitly zeroed.
    - On reset, key_reg and base_reg are reloaded with new secret_key and base without clearing old sensitive contexts.
    - Reset logic initializes sensitive registers with secrets instead of clearing them.
CWE-1247:
    - Security-critical conditions (loop control) and key_reg[0] (conditional multiply) are evaluated in single cycle with no checks, leaving it vulnerable to clock/voltage glitches.
    - No glitch detection, frequency monitor or brownout indication.
    - Asynchronous reset can be glitched to partially load secrets or disturb state
CWE-1256:
    - Interface (secret_key, base, rst_n, clk) has no privilege gating
    - Reset and clock are not protected by any privilege or lifecycle mechanism
CWE-1262:
    - The effective registers (key_reg, base_reg, result) have no access control.
    - No default-deny policy or lock bits
CWE-1300:
    - Control flow is directly key-dependent
    - Loop iterations depends on the bit pattern of key_reg
    - Operand activity is driven by secret-controlled conditions.
